# Blog Cover Direction

Visual direction for Behavera blog covers generated via `gpt-image-1.5`.

## Goal

Create cover images that feel native to Behavera's premium purple visual system and credible for Czech CEOs, HR leaders, and managers in 2026.

## What To Optimize For

- Fast comprehension in blog cards and social previews.
- Executive trust over startup flashiness.
- Authentic human situations over generic stock-photo theater.
- Visual calm that fits the current site gradients, cards, and typography.
- Editorial consistency across the whole set.
- Hyper-realistic documentary feel over polished corporate ad aesthetics.

## Site-Specific Fit

From the current codebase:

- Brand palette is deep plum / violet with soft lavender backgrounds.
- Blog uses dark featured cards, white article cards, rounded corners, and subtle shadows.
- Headlines are bold and high-contrast, so the image should leave usable negative space.
- Overall tone is premium, strategic, calm, and slightly dark rather than playful.

## People

- Czech white professionals only.
- Ages 28-55.
- Men and women should alternate as the main subject across the set for roughly balanced representation.
- Natural-looking faces, realistic proportions, minimal makeup, no model energy.
- Smart casual styling, modern but understated.

## Environment

- Modern Czech office interiors with glass partitions, wood accents, and plants.
- Prague coworking or startup-office feel, but still polished.
- No American visual cues.
- No English slogans or obvious office-wall text.
- Prague skyline through windows is welcome when it supports the scene.

## Image Rules

- Use realistic Central European office environments and believable people.
- Prefer thoughtful expressions, real work moments, and quiet tension over exaggerated emotion.
- Keep composition clean with one strong focal point.
- Make the subject readable at thumbnail size.
- Favor warm whites, graphite neutrals, muted violets, and restrained contrast.
- Use soft natural window light with an editorial 85mm shallow-depth-of-field look.
- Keep color temperature slightly warm, never heavily filtered.
- Preserve skin texture, small imperfections, and lived-in office details.
- Let scenes feel observed, not art-directed.

## Avoid

- Handshakes, staged applause, fake team cheering.
- Hyper-saturated blue-orange corporate palettes.
- Obvious stock-photo smiles.
- Busy scenes with multiple competing subjects.
- Any readable UI, dashboards, logos, or text.
- American or Asian faces.
- Heavy HDR or exaggerated clarity.
- AI artifacts such as broken hands or smeared faces.
- Glossy corporate campaign energy.

## Research Inputs

- Nielsen Norman Group: scannable web content and clarity-first information design.
- HubSpot: content experience and B2B content design that supports trust and brand consistency.
